Tractive System Active Lamp (TSAL)

  • Project led by Lorenzo Kinnicutt, a Ph.D. candidate at Boston University.
  • Time Commitment: September 2021 - January 2022 (In-person)
  • Skills: Prototyping, Python, MATLAB, CAD (Solidworks)
  • Summary:
    • Tractive System Active Lamp, also known as TSAL, is an indicator for electric vehicle cars that indicate the tractive system is active in high voltage or active in low voltage or even itâ€™s off.
    • As a Formula Hybrid Rules, the light has to be bright enough to be visible to everyone even on a sunny day and flash continuously with a frequency between 2 Hz and 5 Hz.

September 2021 - October 2021

  • After testing four different circuit designs using Circuit Simulator, we have decided to use the 555 timers as it is the most useful precision timing device.

October 2021 - December 2021

  • We designed the circuit using a breadboard to test the light. After thorough testing, our TSAL flashed continuously with a frequency between 3Hz without any problems.

December 2021 - January 2022

  • Once we were confident with our design, we began to design the board. We first drew on Schematic edit exactly as we made on a breadboard to re-check any error with the connection.

January 2022

  • We then began to connect circuits on the PCB board to finalize the board. There were a few warnings, so we fixed and solved problems after more testing.
