About Me!

I am a rising junior at Boston University, majoring in Computer Engineering.
My research interests lie in Robotics and Machine Learning.
Below, I have briefly shared my key journey and future aspirations~

1. Robotics + Medical Tech
In my freshman year, I joined the Morphable Biorobotics Lab, where I was introduced to surgical robotics research, sparking my interest in medical technology. Although the work was primarily in Mechanical and Electrical Engineering, I feel it helped me build a solid foundation in robotics.

2. Medical Tech + Software
In my sophomore year, I joined the Kolachalama Lab to expand my knowledge of software engineering in the medical field. There, I worked on a dementia analysis project, primarily using Python for data analysis and software development. It was an amazing experience and learned a lot; however, I realized that I enjoy working on robotics the most.

3. Robotics + Software
After my sophomore year, I sought to integrate both robotics and software engineering. This led me to join the Soft Robotics & Bionics Lab, where I worked as a software engineer, focusing on robotics simulations. Soon, I realized this was the field I wanted to continue in. However, it also made me question‚Äîwhat if I could combine my passion for robotics with my ongoing interest in the medical field? That‚Äôs when I began envisioning a future in medical robotics, where I could work on software that directly contributes to medical advancements.

4. Next Step: Robotics + Software + Medical Tech
Now, I aspire to work at the intersection of software engineering and medical robotics, combining my passion for robotics, software, and the medical field into one cohesive pursuit.
